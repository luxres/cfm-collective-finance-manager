Yes, that's the core of it. Let me walk through the lifecycle concretely, because the direction of the link and what stays untouched both matter.
Step 1 — nothing changes here. The Verein buys the laptop cart. Someone uploads a normal outgoing Receipt, it gets settled with a Transaction/TransactionLine exactly like today. The cash movement is tracked exactly as before — that part of your system doesn't need to know or care that this particular payment happens to be for a depreciable asset.
Step 2 — the deliberate step. A treasurer looks at that receipt and decides "this is capital equipment with a multi-year useful life, not a one-off expense" — that's a judgment call the system can't make automatically (a €3,000 laptop cart and a €3,000 one-off event rental look identical as a Receipt). So they create an Asset entry and link it to that Receipt. The link is a FK on Asset pointing to Receipt — one direction only. The receipt doesn't need to know it has an asset attached; the asset knows which receipt paid for it.
I'd make that FK optional, for two reasons:

Historical assets — equipment the Verein already owned before this system existed, with no receipt in your database at all.
Donated assets — someone gives the Verein a piece of equipment for free. No cash ever moved, so there's no Receipt/TransactionLine, but it still needs to be depreciated for correct accounting.

Step 3 — the asset carries its own facts. Acquisition cost and date can default from the linked receipt (editable, in case the receipt amount includes something the asset cost shouldn't, or vice versa), but useful life and method are things only the asset itself knows — a receipt has no concept of "3 years."
Step 4 — from then on, the asset is independent. Every year, the exporter asks each asset "what's your depreciation for this year?" and adds that as a synthetic row. The original receipt and its transaction are never touched or hidden anywhere else in the app — receipt views, account balances, everything stays exactly as it is today. Only the EÜR export's calculation treats a receipt differently once it notices it's linked to an asset (per your earlier answer: it excludes that receipt's cash row from the deductible-expense side and uses the AfA row instead).
Where the link gets created in the UI, following your existing patterns: a button on the receipt detail page, something like "Als Anlagevermögen erfassen" ("register as capital asset"), that opens a form pre-filled with the receipt's amount/date/tax_category and just asks for useful life — the same shape as your existing "add transaction from receipt" flow.
